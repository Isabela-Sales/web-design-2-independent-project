Easy Ways to Go

To know what to write on the supermarket list for a cool dinner, you need to start somewhere. These are some error-proof combinations to receive your guests and put everybody in a good mood.

  • Pasta Puttanesca + Barbera

    The wine’s acidity is a perfect match for the acidic tomatoes in this pasta. Also, the salty pantry staples in the Pasta Puttanesca will balance the wine’s high acidity. Barbera’s tart cherry and dried herb flavours will support this deliciously simple pasta perfect for any night of the week.

    Recipe

  • Southwest Sweet Potatoes + Zinfandel

    Zinfandel has just enough tannins and body to pair with the rich sweet potato without overpowering the dish. The wine’s jammy flavours will complement the sweetness of the potato, and the smoky aroma will match the stuffing’s southwestern spices.

    Recipe

  • Garlic Herb White Bean Soup + Cinsault

    The fruit notes in Cinsault are a nice balance to the soup’s garlicky flavours while the florals complement the soup’s dried herbs. The light body and tannin of Cinsault are perfect for the simple flavours of the white bean soup.

    Recipe

  • Tortilla Española + Cava

    The savoury nature of the dish highlights the quince, apple, and citrus flavours of the Cava. If possible, reach for a Cava Reserva, which spends a minimum of 15 months on the lees, offering more texture and added complexity.

    Recipe

  • Shakshuka + Nerello Mascalese

    The wine’s acidity is high enough to meet the acidity of the tomato sauce. Plus, the low tannins and light body will not overpower the lightness of an egg-based dish.

    Recipe

  • Grilled PB&J + Rosé Port

    The strawberry, raspberry, and caramel flavours of a Rosé Port perfectly complement a PB&J sandwich’s flavours. Plus, the Port’s fruit notes enliven strawberry or raspberry jam, while the wine’s caramel aromas work well with the salty peanut butter. A Rosé Port also offers enough sweetness to match the PB&J.

    Recipe

If you already passed the first step and want to dare a little more, here are some tips to pick your own paring.

  • Harmonization by similarity

    You should look for similarities between dish and wine. For example, a sweet wine aside a chocolate dessert, or a heavy recipe with a bodied red wine.

  • Harmonization by contrast

    One characteristic balances other on this harmonization. Fat flavour can be neutralized with tannins, once it has astringent effect inside our mouth.

  • Harmonization by complementation

    What lacks in one item is complemented by the other. The Japanese culinary has many dishes with raw neutral fish that can be pared with aromatic white wines, bringing more complexity to the meal.
